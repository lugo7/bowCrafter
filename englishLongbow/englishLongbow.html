English Longbow

Info

The English Longbow is a medieval variant of the longbow used by the English, and Welsh. It was used to great effect during the beginning of the Hundred Years’ War, particularly. The earliest longbow known from England dates to 2665 BC, although the longbow was dominant circa 1250–1450 AD. A longbow must be long enough to allow its user to draw the string to a point on the face or body, and the length therefore varies with the user; beyond this, little agreement can be had about the proper size range. Accounts, and estimates give a range anywhere between 3.9ft(1.2m) – 6ft 11in (2.11m).

What you need

The preferred material to make the longbow was yew, although ash, elm and other woods were also used.

Other suitable woods

  • Hickory
  • Osage Orange
  • Maple
  • Oak
  • Palm
  • A splitting wedge
  • A hammer to drive it
  • A sharp machete or Hachet
  • A pocket knife
  • Draw knife
  • Cabinet scraper
  • Tillering stick
